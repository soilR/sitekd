This summary of the history of redox measurements in soils is based on my own findings and literature I had cited previously. Please give me a note and I will edit the timeline with further findings. For a better overview, I built groups of the commonly employed methods to characterize the soil redox status:

  • EH measurements by redox electrodes
  • Dyes that indicate the presence of, e.g., Fe2+
  • IRIS tools (Indicator of Reduction In Soils) that indicate Mn and Fe reducing conditions by dissolution of synthetic minerals
  • Others (e.g.)
    • Iron metal rods
    • Terminal electron accepting processes (TEAPs) approach
